Q2. What are the filters used for the Inhibitors?

Molecular Descriptors:
- RoF = Lipinski's rule of five (number of violation).
- LogP = Log of Partition Coefficient.
- TPSA = Topological Polar Surface Area.
- HBA = number of Hydrogen Bond Acceptor.
- HBD = number of Hydrogen Bond Donor.
- NRB = Number of Rotatable Bonds.
The average values considered for the descriptors were as follows:
- Have a molecular weight (MW) between 309 and 617 Da (average of 463.3)
- Have a ClogP (calculated with RDKit) between 1.4 and 6.7 (average of 4.0)
- Contain between 0 and 4 hydrogen bond donors (HBD) (average of 2.1)
- Contain between 3 and 11 hydrogen bond acceptors (HBA) (average of 6.7)

Q4. What do the terms SMILES and InCHI refer to?

SMILES (Simplified Molecular Input Line Entry System) is a chemical notation that allows a user to represent a chemical structure in a way that can be used by the computer.

The IUPAC InCHI (International Chemical Identifier) is a textual identifier for chemical substances which are designed to provide a standard way to encode molecular information and to facilitate the search for such information in databases and on the web.

Q5. What are the chromosome X,chromosome Y & chromosome MT refer to?

The X & Y chromosome are the two sex chromosomes.Females have two X chromosomes, while males have one X and one Y chromosome.
MT chromosome is a small circular chromosome composed of mitochondrial DNA usually found inside mitochondria.
